Tunisia

Six times, in 1978, 1998, 2002, 2006, 2018, and 2022, Tunisia took part in the FIFA World Cup. On each of these occasions, Tunisia has played 18 games, winning three, drawing five, and losing 10. They have never been able to proceed past the group stage.

The Tunisian player with the most goals in the competition is Wahbi Khazri, who scored three of them—two in 2018 and one in 2022.

Ghana

Ghana has been qualified to play in Four FIFA World Cup tournaments—2006, 2010, 2014, and 2022. They were the only African team to make it through the group stages of the FIFA World Cup in 2006, and they did so as the continent's sixth consecutive nation. With an average age of 23 years and 352 days, Ghana's team was the youngest at the FIFA World Cup 2006 and received praise for their improving play.

Ghana was placed 13th by FIFA out of the 32 nations that took part in the competition. Ghana advanced past the group stages and made it as far as the quarterfinals before being defeated by Uruguay the 2010 World Cup.
